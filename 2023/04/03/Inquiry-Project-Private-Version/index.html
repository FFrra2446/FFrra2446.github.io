Changsha, China

Today I am going to introduce my hometown Changsha. First, I am going to introduce some foods.

This is Stinky tofu. And I think Stinky tofu isn’t stinky at all. It is not as bad as you think. By the way, I love it. And I found a story about Stinky tofu.

Stinky Tofu’s story

According to legend, in the eighth year of Kangxi in the Qing Dynasty, Wang Zhihe, an examinee who came to Beijing from Huangshan, Anhui, failed to pass the exam. I took the exam for the first time, but it was too far away from the next exam. I have no choice but to stay in the capital and work. Wang Zhihe‘s family is far from wealthy. His father opened a tofu workshop in his hometown. Wang Zhihe learned how to make tofu when he was young, so he rented a few rooms near the Anhui Guild Hall, bought some simple utensils, and ground them a few times a day. Tofu with litres of beans is sold along the street. It’s summer, and sometimes the leftover tofu becomes mouldy and can’t be eaten, but I don’t want to throw it away. He thought hard about the countermeasures, cut the tofu into small pieces, dried them for a while, found a small vat, and salted them.

This one is Spicy crayfish. As the word suggests, it is spicy, and Changsha people are very fond of chillies. (I guess that’s why I love spicy food, too) But I’m not too fond of crayfish, shrimp, or prawns.

I never eat spicy crayfish before. So I’m not sure if it’s delicious or not. Guess what? There’s no story about spicy crayfish. Ok, let’s go to the next one.

This is Fish Head with Chopped Pepper. Its English name is weird. But don’t worry, if you translate it back, it will be correct, so Chinese people can understand you. My father loves this. Whenever we went to a Hunan restaurant, he ordered this. It was good.

糖油粑粑

When I translate this into English, google translate shows me Sugar oil cakes. It’s not a kind of cake. So I just type its Chinese name, 糖油粑粑.

糖油粑粑 is one of the few Changsha foods that do not contain chilli. I never try it before, but my sister Yufei said it was great o((>ω< ))o. 糖油粑粑 are sweet and soft.

Mao Zedong’s life

Mao Zedong was a Chinese revolutionary and political leader who was instrumental in founding the People’s Republic of China in 1949. He was a founding member of the Chinese Communist Party and rose to prominence through his leadership of the Red Army during the Chinese Civil War.

As China’s first chairman, Mao implemented a range of policies, including the collectivization of agriculture, industrialization, and the Cultural Revolution. While some policies were successful, others, such as the Great Leap Forward and Cultural Revolution, were not a good idea.

Despite his mixed legacy, Mao remains a revered figure in China and continues to have an impact on Chinese politics and society.

Mount Yuelu

Mount Yuelu

Mount Yuelu is a cultural and natural attraction located in Changsha. At 300 meters above sea level, the peak offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The mountain is home to several historic landmarks, including the Yuelu Academy, a centre of learning since the Tang Dynasty, and the Lushan Temple, a beautifully restored Buddhist temple. Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy numerous hiking trails, such as the famous Tianma Peak and Yunlu Lake. Yuelu Mountain is a unique and unforgettable destination that blends natural beauty, history, and outdoor activities.
